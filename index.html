01 — The thesis

      
Computers store data. Brains make meaning.

      

        

          For 50 years the operating system metaphor hasn't changed: a desktop, files, folders,
          a trash can. It mirrors a filing cabinet, not a mind. Meanwhile the
          most powerful computer most of us touch every day — the one between our ears — runs on
          a completely different design: associative memory, predictive reasoning, salience-based
          attention, and trained reflex.
        

        

          NEUROS is a thought experiment made real: what if the OS was modeled on
          neurology instead of stationery? AI becomes the cortex. Your notes become
          living memories that resurface when relevant. Tasks aren't files you open — they're
          impulses the system routes for you. The machine stops being a tool you operate and
          becomes a mind you collaborate with.
        

      

    


    
    

      
02 — Architecture

      
Four regions. One coherent mind.

      
Each NEUROS subsystem is modeled on a real structure of the brain — not as decoration, but as a design principle for how it behaves.

      

        

          
🧩

          
Hippocampus

          
Memory & recall

          
An associative memory that doesn't make you search. It surfaces the right note, file or conversation the moment context makes it relevant.

        

        

          
⚡

          
Cortex

          
Reasoning & language

          
The AI reasoning core. It plans, drafts, explains and decides — the deliberate, conscious layer you actually talk to.

        

        

          
🎯

          
Amygdala

          
Salience & priority

          
An attention engine that weighs what matters now. It elevates the urgent, mutes the noise, and protects your focus like an instinct.

        

        

          
🌀

          
Cerebellum

          
Reflex & automation

          
Learned, automatic actions. Repeat something three times and NEUROS offers to make it muscle memory — running it without being asked.
